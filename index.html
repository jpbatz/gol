Welcome to Yet Another Conway's Conway's Game of Life (YAC CGoL) simulation model.

CGol is a cellular automaton - "one of a set of units in a mathematical model that have simple rules governing their replication and destruction. They are used to model complex systems composed of simple units such as living things or parallel processors."

It consists of cells that live or die according to the following rules:

1. A live cell which has 2 or 3 live neighbors continues on to the next generation.

2. A live cell which has 0 or 1 live neighbor, dies of isolation.

3. A live cell which has 4 or more live neighbors dies of overcrowding.

4. A dead cell with exactly 3 live neighbors comes to life in the next generation.

Each cell's immediate neighbors is examined, and their fate in the next generation is automagically determined by the application. All you have to do is provide a seed pattern and the application does all the work.

There are 3 modes to choose for your starting seed pattern. Select one, enter your desired parameters (or accept the defaults), press Start to begin the simulation.

Observe the interesting (and not so eventful), chaotic, or pleasing patterns that may emerge from generation to generation, based on your initial seed pattern.

A simulation can end in one of 3 ways:

1) Still-Lifes are non-terminating stable patterns for which you should press the Done button to stop calculations.

2) Oscillators are non-terminating patterns which will repeat indefinitely. Enjoy the show, and press Done when desired.

3) Terminating pattern - all cells die off, and the grid goes blank. No further action is required.

You can Pause and Resume as you wish.

Please press Done to reload the page after each simulation.
