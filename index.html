Welcome to Yet Another Conway's Conway's Game of Life (YAC CGoL) simulation model.

CGol is a cellular automaton - "one of a set of units in a mathematical model that have simple rules governing their replication and destruction. They are used to model complex systems composed of simple units such as living things or parallel processors."


The model consists of cells that live or die according to the following Guidelines:

1. CELL IN BALANCE ~ A live cell which has 2 or 3 live neighbors continues on to the next generation.

2. CELL OUT OF BALANCE ~ A live cell which has 0 or 1 live neighbor, dies of isolation.

3. CELL IN CHAOS ~ A live cell which has 4 or more live neighbors dies of overcrowding.

4. THRIVING CELL ~ A dead cell with exactly 3 live neighbors comes to life in the next generation.

Each cell's immediate neighbors are examined, and that cell's fate in the next generation is automagically determined by the application, according to the guidelines above. All you have to do is provide a seed pattern and the application does all the work.

There are 3 modes to choose for your starting seed pattern:

Random Mode - Creates a default 5x5 grid with a random seed pattern to run at 100ms per generation. Enter your desired Height, Width, or Speed. Press Start to begin your simulation.

Manual Mode - Challenge yourself to create meaningful or chaotic simulations with seed patterns of your choosing. Creates a default 5x5 grid to be run at 100ms per generation. Enter your desired Height, Width, or Speed. Click on cells to toggle them to a live state. Press Set-Seed to accept your seed pattern. Then press Start to begin the simulation.

Catalog Mode - Select sample seed patterns by categories. Then press Start to begin the simulation.

Tip: Enter greater Speeds (smaller values) for larger grid sizes for observing, and lower Speeds (larger values in the thousands) for closer examination.

Press Pause and Run (Resume) as you wish, to examine the progression of your simulation.

Press Done to end non-terminating simulations and to reload the page after each simulation.

Observe the interesting (and sometimes, not so eventful), chaotic, or pleasing patterns that may emerge from generation to generation, based on your initial seed pattern.

A simulation can end in one of 3 ways:

1) Still-Lifes are non-terminating stable patterns for which you should press the Done button to stop the calculations.

2) Oscillators are non-terminating patterns which will repeat indefinitely. Enjoy the show, and press Done when desired.

3) Terminating pattern - all cells die off, and the grid goes blank. No further action is required.

The challenge is to come up with a seed pattern to create a simulation of your liking in the Manual mode. You may find some good ideas by observing simulations in the Random and Catalog modes.
